# Cold Email Schematic — Shopify Hybrid App (£3,950)

## PURPOSE
This document is a structured schematic for generating personalised cold emails pitching a hybrid mobile app build to Shopify store owners. Feed this to an AI with a list of prospect names, store names, and niches to generate batches of emails at scale.

---

## PRODUCT FACTS (Do not alter)
- Wraps the client's existing Shopify store — no rebuild, no migration
- Native feel via CSS injection (header/footer hidden, native bottom nav)
- Firebase push notifications auto-triggered by Shopify events: order confirmed, shipped, abandoned cart
- Native Notification Inbox (persists messages even with notifications off — required for App Store approval)
- App published under client's own brand on App Store and Google Play
- Industry benchmark: app users have 3–5× higher repeat purchase rate vs. mobile web
- Retainer – Essential: £49/month (keep app live, OS updates, Firebase maintenance)
- Retainer – Growth: £149/month (Essential + monthly push campaigns, abandoned cart sequences, monthly report)
- Price: £3,950 one-off build fee

---

## TONE & STYLE RULES
- UK English throughout
- Direct, commercially savvy — no "I hope this finds you well"
- No bullet lists in the body copy
- Lead with retention pain, not features
- Frame £3,950 as an investment with ROI angle
- Retainer mention: 1–2 sentences max, soft and optional
- Single CTA: book a 15-minute call / live demo
- P.S. line: urgency OR social proof (never both — pick one)

---

## EMAIL STRUCTURE

### Subject Line
- Under 8 words
- Curiosity-driven — focus on the pain (losing repeat buyers, mobile web leaking customers) or the opportunity (app users buy more)
- Do NOT mention price, "free", or agency name
- Generate 3 options per prospect

**Examples:**
- "Your Shopify store is leaking repeat buyers"
- "App users buy 4× more — here's why yours don't have one"
- "£3,950 to stop losing customers to mobile web"

---

### Opening Line (1 sentence)
- Name the retention problem specific to Shopify mobile web
- Optionally acknowledge the prospect's store or niche if known
- No compliments, no preamble

**Template pattern:**
> Most [niche] stores convert well on first visit. The problem is getting that customer back.

---

### Core Pitch (2–3 short paragraphs, 80–110 words total)
1. **What it is** — briefly describe the app shell concept (wraps their existing store, no rebuild, native feel, push notifications from Shopify events, notification inbox)
2. **Why it matters** — cite the 3–5× repeat purchase stat, frame as a retention layer not a rebuild
3. **The investment framing** — £3,950 positioned as ROI, not cost. One recovered customer segment / one reactivated cohort pays it back.

---

### Retainer Soft-Sell (1–2 sentences, optional tone)
- Mention retainers exist from £49/month for ongoing maintenance and growth campaigns
- Do NOT list tiers, do NOT push hard — make it feel like an afterthought

**Example:**
> If you want ongoing push campaigns or abandoned cart sequences after launch, we offer monthly retainers from £49 — but that's entirely your call.

---

### CTA (1 sentence)
- Single ask: a 15-minute call for a live demo
- Phrased as a low-commitment question, not a demand

**Example:**
> Worth a 15-minute call to see a live demo?

---

### Sign-off
- First name, agency name, contact link (Calendly or phone)
- No long sign-off blocks

---

### P.S. Line (choose one type per email, do not mix)

**Scarcity variant:**
> P.S. We're currently onboarding [N] UK stores this quarter — slots are limited before our next build window opens in [Month].

**Social proof variant:**
> P.S. Our last build went live in 23 days and the client saw a 28% increase in returning customers within 60 days.

## AI GENERATION INSTRUCTIONS

When generating emails from this schematic, you will be given:
- `{{first_name}}` — prospect's first name
- `{{store_name}}` — their Shopify store name
- `{{niche}}` — their product niche (e.g. skincare, pet accessories, clothing)
- `{{ps_type}}` — either "scarcity" or "social_proof"
- `{{build_window_month}}` — next month to use in scarcity P.S. (e.g. "May")

Rules for generation:
1. Personalise the opening line to reference the niche or store where natural
2. Do not invent stats beyond those listed in PRODUCT FACTS
3. Keep body copy between 120–160 words
4. Generate exactly 3 subject line options
5. Do not use bullet points anywhere in the email body
6. Output in plain text, ready to paste into an email client

## FUNNEL CONTEXT (Do not include in cold emails)
Cold email → reply / call booked → 15-min live demo call → client onboarding form sent → project kickoff

The Google Form / onboarding questionnaire is sent AFTER the prospect has agreed to proceed — not in the cold email. Including a form link in a cold email signals a template blast and kills reply rates.
