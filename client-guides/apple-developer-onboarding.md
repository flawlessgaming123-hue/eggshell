## How to Register Your UK Company on the Apple Developer Programme and Invite Your Developer

This guide walks you through every step of joining Apple's Developer Programme as a UK limited company, and then giving your developer the access they need to get to work. Don't worry if this is new territory — each step is clearly explained, and we'll flag exactly what you'll need along the way.

---

### Before You Start

Make sure you have all of the following to hand before you begin. Having these ready will save you from stopping halfway through.

- ✅ **An Apple ID** — the email address and password you use for any Apple service (iCloud, App Store, etc.). If you don't have one, create a free account at [appleid.apple.com](https://appleid.apple.com) before starting. Use a company email address, not a personal one.
- ✅ **Your D-U-N-S number** — a unique 9-digit reference number that identifies your business. Apple uses this to verify that your company is a legal entity. You can look yours up for free at [developer.apple.com/enroll/duns-lookup](https://developer.apple.com/enroll/duns-lookup). If your company doesn't have one yet, you can request it through the same page — allow up to 5 business days.
- ✅ **Your company's legal details** — the full registered company name (exactly as it appears on Companies House), your registered address, and your company phone number.
- ✅ **A credit or debit card** — to pay the annual fee of £99 (inc. VAT). Apple accepts Visa, Mastercard, and American Express.
- ✅ **Legal authority to sign on behalf of the company** — Apple will ask you to confirm you are authorised to enter into legal agreements for the business. As the company director, you are.
- ✅ **Your developer's email address** — the address they've asked you to use so they can accept the invitation.

---

#### Step 1: Start Your Enrolment as an Organisation on the Apple Developer Website

Head to [developer.apple.com/programs/enroll](https://developer.apple.com/programs/enroll) and sign in with your company Apple ID. Once signed in, you'll be asked what type of account you'd like to create. Choose **"Organisation"** — not "Individual". The Organisation option is for companies and allows you to add team members (like your developer) later. The Individual option ties the account to one person only and cannot be converted.

📸 *[Screenshot: The enrolment type selection screen showing two options — "Individual" and "Organisation" — with "Organisation" highlighted or selected]*

---

#### Step 2: Complete the D-U-N-S Number Check and Company Verification

On the next screen, enter your D-U-N-S number when prompted. Apple will use this to automatically pull up your registered company details and check they match. Review the details on screen carefully — your company name must match exactly what is registered with Companies House (including "Ltd" or "Limited"). If anything looks wrong, do not proceed; contact Apple Developer Support to correct it first. Once confirmed, Apple will review your information, which typically takes one to five business days. You'll receive an email when this stage is complete.

📸 *[Screenshot: The organisation details form showing fields for D-U-N-S number, company name, address, and phone number, all filled in]*

---

#### Step 3: Pay the Annual £99 Apple Developer Programme Fee

Once Apple has verified your company, you'll receive an email inviting you to complete your enrolment. Follow the link in that email, sign back in, and you'll be taken to the payment screen. Enter your credit or debit card details and confirm the payment of £99. This fee renews every year on the same date, so make a note of it. After payment, your account will be activated — usually within a few minutes. You'll get a confirmation email from Apple once your programme membership is live.

📸 *[Screenshot: The Apple Developer Programme payment screen showing the £99 charge, card entry fields, and a "Purchase" or "Complete Enrolment" button]*

---

#### Step 4: Access App Store Connect and Navigate to Users & Access

Open a new browser tab and go to [appstoreconnect.apple.com](https://appstoreconnect.apple.com). Sign in with the same Apple ID you used to enrol. App Store Connect (think of it as your control panel for everything related to publishing apps on the App Store) is where you manage your team members. Once on the homepage, look for the **"Users and Access"** option — it may appear as a tile on the dashboard or in the navigation menu at the top. Click on it to open your team management area.

📸 *[Screenshot: The App Store Connect homepage with the "Users and Access" tile or menu item visible and highlighted]*

---

#### Step 5: Invite Your Developer by Email with the Admin Role Selected

Inside Users & Access, click the blue **"+"** (plus) button to add a new team member. A form will appear — enter your developer's first name, last name, and the email address they've provided. Under the **"Roles"** section, tick **"Admin"**. The Admin role (which gives your developer full access to manage apps, certificates (the digital files Apple uses to authorise your app to run on devices), and team settings on your behalf) is what they need to do their work properly. Double-check the email address is correct, then click **"Invite"**. Your developer will receive an email from Apple and will need to accept the invitation within 30 days.

📸 *[Screenshot: The "Invite User" form in App Store Connect showing the name fields, email address field, and the Roles section with "Admin" ticked]*

---

### What Happens Next

Your developer will receive an invitation email from Apple (sent from `no_reply@email.apple.com` — let them know to check their spam folder just in case). Once they accept, they'll have full Admin access to your Apple Developer account and can begin setting up and publishing your app. The whole process from here is in their hands. If they need anything from you along the way — such as approving a new app submission or updating billing details — they'll be in touch. Well done for getting this set up!
